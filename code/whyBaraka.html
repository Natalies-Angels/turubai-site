How You Come In

We’d like to build with you

We are Barakoa — a live cultural system built inside Turubai and NUKe.

Barakoa treats sound, movement, space, and visuals as equal languages. For this edition, visuals are not decoration — they are part of the rhythm.

We’re inviting a Visual Digital Design Curator & Manager to help shape how Barakoa is seen, felt, and remembered — before, during, and after the gig.

Context

Barakoa is not just an event. It is a living system — unfolding through sound, movement, space, and image.

Visuals are not added last. They help set tempo, emotional tone, and memory.

This role exists to protect that integrity.

How You Come In

You come in as a curator, not a content executor.

Your role is to:

  • Translate Barakoa philosophy and NiloFunk sound into visual language
  • Hold visual coherence across platforms, moments, and collaborators
  • Guide aesthetic direction rather than chase trends

This is not about volume. It’s about clarity, restraint, and intention.

Where You Come In

1. Pre-Gig — Narrative & Build-Up

  • Shape the visual tone of the Barakoa gig
  • Guide how Barakoa, NiloFunk, and collaborators are introduced
  • Curate key visuals, posters, motion snippets, and social rhythm

2. During the Gig — Live Translation

  • Define how visuals behave during the experience
  • Coordinate screens, lighting direction, and live capture logic
  • Ensure visuals respond to sound, not overpower it

3. Post-Gig — Memory & Documentation

  • Curate edits, stills, and short-form visuals
  • Protect the narrative from shallow recap content

What You Manage

  • Visual rhythm across Barakoa, Zen Keg, and collaborators
  • Alignment between sound, space, and visual language
  • A small, intentional visual pipeline — not chaos

Our Ask

  • 1 intentional visual review deliverable per month
  • 1 final visual output per month
  • 1 alignment meeting per month
  • Commitment to process over hype

This is not a daily-content role.
