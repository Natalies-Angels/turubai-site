How You Come In

Dear Kemboi,
we'd like to build with you

We are Barakoa — a cultural ritual built inside Turubai and NUKe. We work at the intersection of music, theatre, fashion, psychology and underground culture.

We are reaching out to you not to “book a set”, but to help curate an emotional arc. To use sound as structure. Breath. Permission.

Barakoa needs musicians who understand pacing, silence, restraint — and when to let a room open. That’s why you.

What is Barakoa?

Barakoa is a popup immersive experience. A temporary world built from temporary boxes.

It is a space to explore emotional masks — the ways we hide our true feelings from ourselves and others.

Through live music, theatre, fashion, installations, and ritual play, Barakoa creates space to soften what we suppress.

Theme for Barakoa 2026:
Love, Fear & the Quiet.

Why Barakoa?

We live in a generation that feels everything loudly but speaks about nothing honestly.

Barakoa exists to bring awareness to the masks we wear — and let them soften through art.

In a world that prioritizes noise, Barakoa creates space for quiet. In a culture obsessed with spectacle, Barakoa invites restraint. In an age of distraction, Barakoa encourages presence.

Barakoa is a ritual for our times. A space to feel deeply, express honestly, and connect authentically.

Why You, Specifically

For this edition, the Barakoa Beat is NiloFunk.

This is where you come in.

NiloFunk draws from Afro-house and Afro-funktronica, filtered through Nilotic rhythmic intelligence and live orchestration. Cyclical indigenous grooves meet restrained electronic architecture.

It is groove-led, trance-adjacent, and built for collective movement — prioritizing repetition, restraint, and embodied listening over spectacle.

In February, your sound helps us test the foundations of NiloFunk — pacing, arrival energy, and rhythmic restraint.

In June, your role is to hold the rhythmic centre of the day.

We see you as someone that can help us curate a memorable barakoa experience.
