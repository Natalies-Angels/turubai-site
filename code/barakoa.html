BARAKOA IS A MASKERADE

MASKS AS LANGUAGE
THEATRE WITHOUT STAGE
FASHION · PERFORMANCE · RITUAL
ANONYMITY AS FREEDOM
Barakoa is not a party. It is a living maskerade — where fashion, art, theatre and underground sound collapse into one ritual body.

Guests enter masked, not to hide — but to *arrive differently*. Each mask carries a posture, a power, a way of moving through the night.

This gathering opens the doorway into Perpetration Archive (PA) — a deeper mythology of six masks, six forces, and the cultural memory they carry.
